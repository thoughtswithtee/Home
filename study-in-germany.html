Study In Germany



Q: How much does it cost to study in Germany? A: Tuition is generally free. However, you’ll need money to cater to rent, clothing, feeding, basic needs in life. During the visa application, proof of funding to cover the basic needs above for at least one year is usually required for a student visa. This proof of funding is what “the cost associated” to study in Germany. It could ultimately be of no cost to you, it could cost a little or it could cost a lot.

If you secure a scholarship, it would be at no cost to you because even your flight ticket is paid for.
It would cost a little if you possibly know someone who lives in Germany and is willing to stand in as a sponsor and sign a sponsorship letter for you. This letter can be used as your proof of funding and you only have to pay for your flight ticket or other related costs.
It may cost a lot if you are self-sponsored and as such you have to present a blocked account with funds sufficient to cater for you for one year. The amount is usually fixed by German law.

Q: How long is a master’s degree? A: The curriculum of a master’s degree in Germany is generally two years long, in a few cases, it extends for one year. However, there are no strict restrictions, you can do it at your own pace and do as many courses as you can pass in one semester just within reasonable limit, reasonable number of years.

Q: Can I get a scholarship? A: Yes, it is very possible, the DAAD offers scholarships, Erasmus offers scholarships, there is the Conrad Adenauer Scholarship, The Heinrich Boll Scholarship and several others.

Q: How do I get a scholarship? A: You apply for it. Some scholarships have individual applications while for others, the application for admission is done as a single application, along with the scholarship application.

Q: Does my VISA have the limit to leave immediately after studies? A: On first arrival, there is usually a 3- or 6-months Visa and after arrival, this can be extended for a year or 2 years till studies is completed. When the study is completed, you get an 18-month permit that allows you work full time and get a job in your field hopefully. This is slightly different from a student Visa that allows you work part-time i.e. 120 days in a year.

Q: How advisable is it to move to Germany as a single parent? A: As a single parent, generally, it is not easy. Having said that, there is support for females in Germany, there is financial support, there is consideration for “mothers” when making shift plans at the work place. There are policies that support women, so, in Germany, it possibly would be easier for women than in other countries.
