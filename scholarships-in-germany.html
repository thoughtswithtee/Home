Scholarships in Germany Q & A: DAAD



Although tuition in Germany is generally free, you need to cater to your living expenses. This means we have to talk about funding.

Q: Are there open scholarships? What are the deadlines for DAAD scholarships A: Depending on the time of year that you read this, there are still open scholarships. Some DAAD scholarships have a December 15 deadline, other deadlines are 15th January, 31st January, March 15 and June 15. Some deadlines fall on the 31st of these months, others on the 15th.

Q: How can I apply? A: There are different DAAD scholarships. There is the development related scholarship, EPOS and there are several other DAAD scholarships. Usually, for EPOS, you visit the brochure, select the course you want to apply for, click on the attached link and it directs you to the university’s website where you apply for admission to the university. As you apply, you also have the opportunity to include in that application that you are also applying for the DAAD scholarship and then you upload your documents. It is one application which serves both for admission and for scholarship. There are other DAAD scholarships in which the admission application is different from the scholarship application. However, majority of the EPOS scholarships are as described above.
In other cases, its simply an online application and you upload documents similar to that of the admission application.

Q: What are the scholarship requirements? A: Generally, scholarship applications requirements are similar to admission requirements. Additionally, in some cases, references are required and in other cases, research proposals may be required.

Q: Do I need to have a first class? A: No, in many cases you don’t need to have a first class. Interestingly, for most DAAD scholarships, the grade is usually not specified. It follows more that you apply for admission and if you qualify for admission then you meet the academic requirements for the scholarship. In many cases, the DAAD does not impose additional academic requirements. Usually, for EPOS, t he university you apply to for admission recommends you for the DAAD scholarship.

Q: Do I need to pay application fee? A: Usually, No. For DAAD scholarship application, you do not need to pay an application fee. However, it could be possible that you are applying for a DAAD scholarship and the associated university you apply to uses uni-assist to process admission application. If this is the case, it is possible that you pay an application fee. However, some universities wave the uni-assist application fee.

Q: Is there a DAAD scholarship for undergraduates A: I am not aware of any DAAD scholarship for undergraduates. However, please visit www.daad.de to see latest info.
