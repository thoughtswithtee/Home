Storms

...All this running around dey pack water upandan. Na im dey ginger the storm more. In the first place, the storm na to make you sweat. Reason Jesus wey dey this boat...
...Jesus called that little faith. Really?? Me calling on you in a storm is little faith? The only issue with calling on Him in that case was that Jesus was in the boat already. Clearly, they could see Him in their boat. And, He was sleeping. I found my first solution here...

The new testament features two storms. Both storms had Jesus Christ and His disciples in them. As I meditated on the two storm scenarios, solutions became evident to me

In the first, Jesus and His disciples started a boat trip. From the start of the trip, Jesus was in the boat, on the way, a tempest arose with Jesus still in the boat. However, Jesus seemed unbothered as He was recorded to have been fast asleep on a pillow. The disciples were in a total panic, they eventually woke Jesus up and after He stilled the storm, His response to them was “O ye of little faith”. I saw here then that if them being in a panic and waking him up was being of little faith then being of great faith was possibly to trust Him. Be at peace and just trust Him.

To take one look at Jesus, see Him at peace in the storm and then choose also to be at peace in the storm with the confidence that Jesus is on this boat with me, We can’t drown..

In the second scenario, Jesus told them to go on, He’d join them. As they went, a storm arose. From the Mount where Jesus was, He saw them and joined them by foot as He walked on water to them. The whole time Jesus was on water, there was still a storm. Never mind that they were frightened when they saw Him and Peter attempted to walk on water too but didn’t last long at it.

What I saw in this second storm was that Jesus didn’t rebuke this storm, He simply entered their boat and the storm ceased. One small difference between the two storms was that in one, Jesus was in the boat at the start of the storm and in the other He wasn’t in the boat at the start of the journey.

The solutions seemed quite evident then. Cos in the first, the disciples were doing what any sane person would. Try to steady the boat, get the water out, panic a little cos this is real, true danger, call on Jesus, wake Him up. Jesus called that little faith. Really?? Me calling on you in a storm is little faith? The only issue with calling on Him in that case was that Jesus was in the boat already. Clearly, they could see Him in their boat. And, He was sleeping. I found my first solution here. If Jesus was already on your boat when the storm hit then take a cue from Jesus and be at peace. If your chest carry you, sleep even. A deep sleep at that. All you need is confidence in Him and His ability to get you safely to shore. Be at Peace. Your boat won’t capsize with Jesus in it.

The other solution was, if He wasn’t on the boat when the storm hit then you need Him on your boat. His entrance into your boat will introduce all the calm you need. It will bring the peace you need, It will still the storm.

In both storms, when they reached their destination, it was a new territory and demon possessed men were waiting at the shore. Also notice that Jesus rebuked the storm in the first story. Its evil spirits Jesus normally rebukes. How come He seems to be rebuking the seas and they are obeying. We can see that these storms were simply oppositions to getting to the place Jesus has given the instruction that we must go over to.

Friend, are you in a situation that feels just like a storm. Maybe its “turningoniown” in popular Nigerian parlance for “turning on its own” and you really don’t just understand again. Perhaps you need Jesus if you don’t yet have him in your life. He brings peace.

Friend if He was in your life when this storm hit, trust His saving abilities and embrace the peace He has already given. (All this running around dey pack water upandan. Na im dey ginger the storm more. In the first place, the storm na to make you sweat. Reason Jesus wey dey this boat, trust Him, be at peace).

Till I write again, If you did enjoy this blog, please share to someone.
